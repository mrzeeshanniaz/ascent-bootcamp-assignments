Disinformation has four shapes — and one cure

Fabricated

Invented from nothing — fake quote, fake event.

Manipulated

Real media, edited — cropped, slowed, retouched.

Miscontextualized

Real footage, wrong caption — old clip, new lie.

Impersonated

Fake source dressed as a real outlet or person.

Worked example — lateral reading

Claim: “New study proves [policy] doubles teen productivity — TeenScienceDaily.org.”

  • 1Search the site + "funded by" / "owner" — who pays?
  • 2Search the author — real researcher or invented byline?
  • 3Find the actual study — does it exist? say that?
  • 4Does any independent outlet report it?
Vertical reading lies to you: staying on the page to judge the page lets it grade its own homework. The verdict comes from the other tabs.

Two tools that live in the other tabs

🌐

Domain analysis

  • Look-alike domains: bbc-news.co ≠ bbc.com — a swapped letter or extra word.
  • .org is not neutral — anyone can buy one. The ending proves nothing.
  • Read the About / whois page: who owns it, when was it registered, who funds it?
  • Clean design is cheap. Trust the trail, not the typography.
🔁

Reverse image search

  • Right-click → search the image (or drop it into a reverse-search tool).
  • Sort by oldest — find the earliest appearance.
  • Compare the original caption / date / place to the claim in front of you.
  • Catches the #1 fake: a real photo with a brand-new lie attached.
Both answer the same question: where did this actually come from — before someone re-captioned it for you?

Synthetic media — no single signal is enough

🎭

Tells (necessary, never sufficient)

  • Video: lip-sync drift, weird blinking, lighting/shadows that don't match.
  • Audio: too-even cadence, no breaths, flat emotion.
  • AI image: mangled hands/text, melted backgrounds — but generators are getting good fast.
🔖

Content Credentials (C2PA)

  • A provenance tag: what tool made or edited this, and when.
  • Present + valid = supporting evidence.
  • Absent ≠ fake. A watermark can be stripped or forged.
  • A tag is a clue, not a verdict.
Judgment checkpoint: “It looks real” and “it has a watermark” each fail on their own. A pass on one signal is never a verdict — trace the claim chain: who, when, where, corroborated by whom.

Your footprint & your attention

📌

Permanence

Delete removes your copy. Screenshots outlive deletion. Post as if it's forever — because for someone, it is.

🎓

Who's looking

🎰

Attention economy

The feed optimizes for engagement — not truth, not your interest. Engagement ≠ true ≠ good for you.

Private self-audit (NOT submitted, NOT shared): count today's notifications · name your 3 loudest apps · silence one. No one collects this — ask yourself: “who decided I'd look at that?”
THINK LIKE A BUILDER 🛠️

Verifying a claim is debugging

🔁

1Reproduce

Find the original — not the screenshot of a screenshot. Can you get back to the source artifact?

🧵

2Trace the chain

Follow it back: who captured it, when, where, and who else confirms. Walk the stack, frame by frame.

🎯

3Isolate the break

Pinpoint where it fails — fabricated? re-captioned? unsourced? That's your repro of the bug.

Same loop, different domain: a developer doesn't trust “it looks broken” — they reproduce and isolate. You don't trust “it looks real.” This is the Problem-Solving competency.
