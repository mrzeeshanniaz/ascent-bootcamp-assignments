THE HEART OF THE SESSION ♥

Accessibility is a creator skill

🔊

Alt text — describe the information

  • Bad: "chart" · "image1.png" · "logo.jpg 2400x600"
  • Good: "Bar chart: recycling rose 20%→65%, 2020–2025"
  • Decorative divider? Mark empty alt="" — don't read noise
🌓

The other three checks

  • Contrast: light-grey text on white fails tired eyes
  • Heading order: never skip H1 → H3
  • Font size: body ≥ 11–12pt, readable at midnight
Empathy, not a checklist: you're writing for the classmate on a screen reader and the tired human reading late. Describe what a sighted reader gains from the image.

Lock it, then share it carefully 🔒

ScenarioTierWhy

Teacher giving feedback on your draftCommenterSuggests without altering your text

Export a locked PDF for anything final (layout frozen). And: "anyone with the link can edit" = accidental public exposure. Scope it, every time.

AI drafting — disclose or plagiarize? 🤖

SituationVerdictWhy
AI drafts an outline; you write & verify the prose, note "outline drafted with AI"Disclose — OKStructure is a tool; the thinking is yours
AI writes a paragraph; you fact-check, rewrite in your voice, cite the assistDisclose — OKVerified + transformed + disclosed
AI writes the report; you paste it unchanged, unverified, no mentionPlagiarismUnverified AI prose passed as your own
AI invents a "fact" / citation; you submit it uncheckedIntegrity failNo verification = the errors aren't honestly yours
The rule: structure and verification are yours. Disclosed + checked + rewritten = OK. Unverified prose passed off as your own = plagiarism. (More in the Session 6 AI policy.)

  • ≥ one table + one inline image with alt text
  • Export a locked PDF and attach it
  • Share the source at the correct tier (facilitator = Viewer/Commenter)

The grading bar (DOCUMENTS · no secrets)

  • 0 Missing: flat text, no headings; or no PDF/share
  • 1 Emerging: headings skip levels or faked; manual spacing remains
  • 2 Proficient: semantic H1–H3 in order, native breaks, real list + table, locked PDF
  • 3 Mastery: + alt text, accessible contrast & sizes, correct tiers explained
Structure over styling — you grade against this same bar. A pretty wall of bold text is still Level 1.
