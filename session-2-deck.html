Judgment checkpoint: When is the AI answer panel enough — and when do you still need a primary source?

Read a URL like an address 🔍

🔒https://www.gov.uk/bank-accounts?age=16

🔒 What the padlock does promise

  • Encrypts transit — nobody between you and the site can read or alter the page.
  • Proves the domain — you really reached gov.uk, not an impostor in the middle.

🚫 What it does NOT promise

  • That the site is honest, accurate, or safe.
  • A scam site can have a perfect padlock — the lock is about the pipe, not the contents.
scheme (how) · host (who) · path (where on the site) · query (the search after ?) — and the lock secures the trip, not the truth.

Search is a tool — drive it with operators

➕

AND / space

Both terms must appear. Default between words.

🔀

OR

Either term qualifies — widens the net. (bank OR account)

❝❞

"exact phrase"

Locks word order. "minimum age", not "age minimum".

🏛️

site:

Restrict to a domain. site:gov.* = authoritative only.

➖

-minus

Exclude noise. -forum -reddit drops opinion/aggregators.

🎯

Worked example

Task: official minimum age to open a bank account in your country.

"minimum age" (bank OR account) site:gov.* -forum -reddit
Watch it sharpen: the panel gives one number fast; the actual gov page gives the number plus conditions (guardian co-sign, ID) the panel often drops — that dropped caveat is a real discrepancy.

When is the panel enough? The judgment

✅

Panel is enough when…

  • Spelling of a word — stable, low-stakes
  • How photosynthesis works — settled, no decision rides on it
  • Quick gist of a news event — orientation only
📑

Need a primary source when…

  • Current tuition fee — time-sensitive, exact figure
  • A medication dose for a real person — safety-critical, never a panel
  • A statistic going in your assignment — anything quoted/graded must trace to a source
The rule: cheap-and-stable → the panel is fine. Stakes-or-quoted → open the source. Same question as S1: "what's the worst that happens if this is wrong?"

Professional email — who goes where 📧

📌

Subject

Specific + scannable: "Assignment 2 findings — search discrepancy (B. Ahmed)". Topic + whether to act, at a glance.

🎯

To

The action owner — the person who must do something with this.

👀

CC

Informed, not actioned — they need visibility, not a task.

🕶️

BCC

Privacy-protected — recipients can't see this address. Use for large or private lists.

↩️

Reply-all

Helps when everyone needs the update. Noise when it's a thank-you to the whole list.

📎

Attach + .zip

Compress multiple files into one .zip and announce it in the body so it's expected.

Google steps: select files on your computer → right-click → Compress to make the .zip → in Gmail, Compose → paperclip Attach (or insert from Drive) → name the file in the body before sending.
GRAMMAR IS NO LONGER A TELL ⚠️

Spotting AI-written phishing

✉️

1Sender domain

Display name says "School IT" — the real address is it@sch00l-helpdesk.co. Read the address, not the name. Watch for paypa1.com, micros0ft-support.net.

🖱️

2Hover the link

Hover, don't click. The visible text says the portal; the real destination is a stranger. Mismatch = stop.

⏰

3Unexpected urgency

"Verify in 2 hours or lose access." Sudden asks for credentials, payment, or gift cards. Legit orgs don't pressure-rush logins.

The trap: AI now writes perfect English. Don't grade emails on grammar — grade them on domain, hover target, and urgency. Those three still work.

Assignment 2 — search, verify, email

The task

  • Run a boolean search on an approved question
  • Compare the AI panel vs. two primary sources
  • Name one specific discrepancy (a number, date, or dropped caveat)
  • Email the findings formally — CC structure + a working .zip

Competency scored: Verification.

Grading bar (best-evidence, 4 levels)

0 MissingNo search or no comparison; no email/attachment.
1 EmergingChecks the panel only; first claim at face value; no real discrepancy.
2 ProficientLateral reading — two genuine sources, names a real specific discrepancy.
3 Mastery+ traces which source is more authoritative and why, and which to trust.
Pass gate: professional email quality is required — correct CC logic, subject/greeting/sign-off, and a working .zip that opens. Strong verification with a broken email does not pass.

The model email — self-check against this

Subject:

Assignment 2 findings — boolean search discrepancy (B. Ahmed)

To: instructor@school.edu   CC: study-partner@school.edu

Dear Mr. Khan,
Please find my Assignment 2 findings attached. I ran the boolean query, compared the AI panel against two primary sources, and noted one discrepancy where the panel omitted the guardian co-sign requirement.
I've CC'd Sara, my study partner, so she has visibility on the source we shared.
The full notes and screenshots are in the attached compressed file: assignment2-findings.zip.
Thank you,
Bilal Ahmed

  • 1To = action owner (instructor grades it).
  • 2CC = informed (partner shared a source, no task).
  • 3No BCC — nothing private to shield.
  • 4.zip named in the body — the recipient expects it.
Subject + greeting + sign-off + correct CC logic + a working .zip = the pass bar. Match this before you submit.
