A talk is a journey: the CPEC arc

🧭

CContext

Why care? Where are we? Set the scene in one line.

⚠️

PProblem

The tension. What's wrong, and who feels it?

📊

EEvidence

One fact, number, or example. Not five.

🎯

CCall-to-action

What now? Exactly what you want them to do.

One CPEC job per slide. Four moves, four slides — Context → Problem → Evidence → Call-to-action.

The 25-words rule — the cut is the skill

❌ Before (60 words — a wall)

"In our school there is currently a significant and ongoing problem with the amount of food that gets thrown away in the cafeteria every single day, and many students and teachers have noticed this but nobody has actually measured how much food is being wasted or proposed any kind of realistic solution to fix it."

✅ After (14 words — Problem slide)

Our cafeteria wastes food daily.
No one has measured how much — or proposed a fix.

The number and example go on the next slide, not crammed here.

Cap: 25 words per slide. Same meaning, half the words — everything cut was filler.

What do you CUT? Signal vs. noise

📡

Signal — keep

  • The one idea the audience must leave with
  • The single number or example that proves it
  • The specific ask in your Call-to-action
🔇

Noise — cut

  • Hedges & throat-clearing: "currently there is an ongoing…"
  • A second CPEC job jammed onto the slide
  • Decoration, clip-art, full sentences as scripts
The test: read a line aloud — if cutting it doesn't change what they walk away knowing, it's noise. Cut it.

Make it readable — and share it right

🌗

Contrast & access

Dark-on-light or light-on-dark — never grey-on-grey. Aim 4.5:1. Squint test: if the title blurs, it fails. Don't rely on colour alone.

🧲

Group & align

Group related objects so they move as one. Snap-to-grid and alignment guides — nothing 3px off. Tidy layout reads as competence.

🖥️

Share & present

Presenter view = notes for you. Audience sees only the slide. Share one window, not the desktop. Check the preview, camera on.

Wrong share leaks your tabs to 12 people. Share a single window — verify the shared preview before you talk.

A talk is a structured argument

🧩

1Decompose

Break your message into parts. CPEC is decomposition — four named jobs instead of one blur.

➡️

2Sequence

Order the parts so each sets up the next: Context before Problem, Evidence before the ask.

✂️

3Cut

Remove anything that doesn't serve the goal — the same instinct you use to debug code down to what works.

PROBLEM-SOLVING competency: decompose → sequence → cut. Build a talk the way you build a solution.

Live delivery — in your breakout room (the heart)

How it runs

  • 3 rooms of ~4 (pre-assigned, mixed levels)
  • Each student screen-shares + delivers 3 min
  • Peers post 1 structure + 1 delivery note to the shared form
  • ~5 min/talk × 4 ≈ 20 min — fits the window

Online etiquette

  • Mics muted while someone presents
  • Claps in chat (👏 / "🔥 slide 3") — don't unmute
  • Questions held to the feedback slot
  • Facilitator rotates; cloud recording on
The one fix that matters: look at the camera, not the slide. Presenter view is for you — the camera is for your audience.

Assignment 5 + the grading bar

The task

  • Refine & resubmit your 4-slide deck, applying peer feedback
  • Add a 2-sentence note: one piece of feedback you applied + what you changed
  • Scored on structure + contrast, not flair — no points for animations or stock photos
